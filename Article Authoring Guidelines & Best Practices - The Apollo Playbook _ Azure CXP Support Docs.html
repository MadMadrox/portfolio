    Article Authoring Guidelines & Best Practices - The Apollo Playbook

    Overview

    The Apollo playbook provides the resources to help you learn how to create the optimal customer experience when you write your support articles.

    The playbook provides authoring best practices and authoring guidelines for writing style & strategy, formatting, branding, and accessibility in alignment with the Microsoft Style Guide. The authoring guidelines also provide tips for working with Markdown and support for new authors learning about the Elixir→GitHub→Portal publishing experience.

    Apollo articles are part of the Solution 2.0 initiative. Learn more at Solution 2.0 Overview.

    What is an Apollo article?

    Apollo is an insightful, powerful, self-help experience for our customers and support engineers. By introducing a single, customizable canvas, Apollo lets you create a solution that specifically targets the customer's issue and expedites the troubleshooting experience.

    Apollo is a single solution that encompasses all solution types under one canvas, so that you don't need to author diagnostics in a separate config tool.

    Apollo lets you combine text, images, diagnostics, charts, videos, and relevant data to build experiences that truly help customers to solve the issue themselves.

    Apollo solutions are displayed by two specific user actions in the Azure portal:

    • When the user selects Diagnose and Solve under the context of a resource
    • When the user selects a support topic in step 2 of the case submission workflow

    Apollo authoring and publishing process

    To author an Apollo solution:

    1. Start at Elixir→Solution management→Apollo solutions.
    2. Create a new Apollo article, or copy from an existing article in the list.
    3. Fill out the metadata and the content using our authoring guidelines and best practices.
      • Add rich solutions to your Apollo article
        • Copy and paste from templates.
        • Use Add solution to automatically build solution components.
    4. Preview your article before submitting for editorial review.
      • Rich components can be previewed in the portal after the article is submitted for review.
    5. The review team works with a 2-day SLA, but often publish or return an article for correction sooner than 2 days. After some time has passed, check on your article's status in Elixir to see if it was published or needs corrections.
      • The editors may send a request for corrections if your article doesn't meet the Apollo minimum requirements.
      • You may have to correct the article numerous times until it meets the minimum requirements to be published.
    6. After an article is published, the status will change on the table in Elixir.
      • Track the article's SHS rate and adjust as needed based on Bonsai recommendations or the guidance of best practices.

    Validation rules and checklist

    To ensure that your self-help articles meet validation rules in Elixir and the Microsoft style guidelines:

    • Use the latest Apollo template.
    • Follow the Validation rules and checklist.
      • The Validation rules and checklist is a summary of some of the most important aspects of the Authoring guidelines in the Apollo playbook. Review the subpages of this playbook for more details and examples.
      • Examples:
        • Use contractions (Use don't instead of do not; you'll instead of you will).
        • Bold all UI elements. Don't overuse bold in text.
        • Enclose error messages in text in quotation marks
        • Articles must meet the Apollo minimum requirements or they will be rejected by the review team.

    Authoring guidelines

    You can use the subpages in this Apollo playbook to familiarize yourself with authoring best practices, the Apollo minimum requirements, and the concepts of voice, tone, and strategy of the Microsoft writing style. Support for new authors unfamiliar with the publishing process can also be found in the playbook's subpages.
